About Me

I have had a 15-year career as a government policy and finance analyst. During that time, I advised politicians on budgetary and policy matters, worked in public-private partnerships to redevelop blighted communities, and administered programs to provide assistance to low-income families. I am proud of my accomplishments.

But I am now going about public service in a different way.

Among other things, I am the Captain of Code for Sacramento, a community organization dedicated to improving people’s access to government information and services. This involves a lot of fundraising, event organizing, and volunteer herding.

But what I really love is data analysis and visualization. I get a geeky kind of pleasure from turning data into information, information into knowledge, and knowledge into wisdom (a.k.a. climbing the DIKW Pyramid), and using that newly-discovered wisdom to help people. This website collects my explorations into data analysis and visualization for the public good. Enjoy! And let me know what you think!
